Basic Info

Encryption is a part of our everyday life.

From physical locks to digital software, valuable information is stored in various ways.

Computers store information in numbers, and can scramble numbers in order to encode things.

Passwords and other sensitive information are encoded by default, but things like emails are easier to tap into.

That is why there are additional ciphers people use.

History of encryption:

Way back in 50 B.C., Caesar used a cipher in order to send messages to his officers by shifting each letter over 3 letters.

This primitive cipher was easy enough for a person to decode without a device.

Since then, locksmiths have been able to create locks that were precise to the millimeter.

Without the correct key, no one could unlock it.

However, the dawn of World War II led to the creation of a machine that could take in inputs and give an output both ways.

This was the rise of the enigma machine.

It would take the message and encode it to a level where it was impossible for any human to try all the combinations.
